# <p align="center"> What is ReVanced?

**RV**, short for "**R**e**V**anced", and **RVX**, short for "**R**e**V**anced e**X**tended", are modified versions of the YT and YT Music apps that allow you to add many features not available natively, such as SponsorBlock (which skips sponsored content, intros, outros, and more) or Return YT Dislike (which restores the dislike counter on videos, making it easier to assess video quality). These mods can block ads, hide Shorts, remove the create button, change the home page, and much more, with each feature being toggleable so you can customize your experience.

# <p align="center"> History of ReVanced :

YT Vanced was an app that allowed users to enjoy some YT Premium features, such as ad blocking and Picture-in-Picture, as well as extra features like SponsorBlock and, in its final versions, Return YT Dislike (RYD). However, on March 13, 2022, the Vanced team announced the end of support for YT Vanced after receiving a cease-and-desist letter from Google. The team stated that existing installations would continue to work for a while but would eventually become obsolete without updates, which is now the case.

A few days later, on March 21, 2022, after the end of Vanced was announced, the ReVanced project started as an open-source effort to provide similar functionality through user-applied patches instead of distributing modified APKs. Modifying an app for personal use is generally allowed, whereas sharing modified builds can raise legal issues, which is what caused problems for Team Vanced. ReVanced’s approach is to provide patching tools and patch sets, without distributing pre-patched apps, which is intended to keep the project on safer legal ground.

After several months of development, ReVanced released its first public patches in 2022 and soon added support for YT Music, then for other apps. Today, YT Vanced is no longer maintained and is considered insecure and outdated, while ReVanced and its forks support current versions of YT and remain actively developed as open-source projects.

# <p align="center"> As for Extended :

Before Vanced was discontinued, the developer Inotia00 had already provided additional features on top of Vanced under the name ([Vanced Extended](https://t.me/vanced_mod_archive)) distributing builds via Telegram. After Vanced development stopped and ReVanced appeared, Inotia00 adapted his work into the open-source project ReVanced Extended, based on the ReVanced patches. ReVanced Extended offers a larger set of patches but focuses mainly on YT, YT Music, and, more recently, Reddit, while maintaining all the core features of Vanced and adding options that track new YT changes.

Although its community is smaller than the main ReVanced project, Inotia00 has set up translation infrastructure (for example with Crowdin) so that the community can help translate YT Extended and YT Music Extended into multiple languages, including the new settings added by the patches.

# <p align="center"> And now, Morphe App :

After the discontinuation of ReVanced Extended (RVX), some of its main contributors, including Inotia00, shifted their focus to a new project called **Morphe** (From Greek morphḗ (« shape/form »), referencing Morphée (god of dreams who shapes dream forms)—symbolizing app transformation via patches, like reshaping reality in a dream, into something better). Morphe is presented as a modern alternative to ReVanced (and RVX), developed by former ReVanced/Extended contributors who decided to continue their work under a different project structure and governance.

Morphe works in a similar way to ReVanced: it is an app patcher for Android that lets you choose which patches to apply to supported apps, then installs a modified version with the selected features. For YT, Morphe offers an ad‑free experience, background and Picture‑in‑Picture playback without Premium, SponsorBlock integration, Return YT Dislike, UI customization, and many other options comparable to or derived from RVX patches. It aims to provide fast updates, stability improvements, and a familiar configuration experience for users coming from Vanced, ReVanced, or RVX.

The Morphe project maintains its own website and patching tools, and positions itself as a community‑driven, open‑source‑oriented continuation of what ReVanced Extended offered, while avoiding distributing pre‑patched APKs directly, in line with the “patch‑it‑yourself” model introduced by ReVanced.
